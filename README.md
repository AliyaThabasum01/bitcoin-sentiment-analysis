# Bitcoin Market Sentiment vs Trader Performance Analysis

## Objective

Analyze the relationship between Bitcoin market sentiment (Fear & Greed Index) and trader performance using Hyperliquid historical trading data.

## Datasets

1. Bitcoin Fear & Greed Index Dataset
2. Historical Trader Data (Hyperliquid)

## Methodology

* Converted timestamps into dates.
* Merged trader records with daily market sentiment.
* Analyzed trade volume, profit/loss, and win rates across sentiment categories.

## Key Findings

### Trading Activity

* Fear periods contained the highest trading activity with over 133,000 trades.
* Greed periods accounted for over 36,000 trades.

### Profitability

* Greed sentiment generated the highest average profit per trade (~87.89).
* Fear sentiment generated an average profit of ~50.05 per trade.

### Win Rate

* Extreme Greed produced the highest win rate (~49%).
* Fear and Greed periods maintained win rates above 40%.
* Neutral sentiment showed the weakest performance.

## Insights

1. Traders tend to perform better during Greed phases.
2. Fear markets attract significantly more trading activity.
3. Extreme Greed conditions appear to improve trade success rates.
4. Neutral markets provide fewer profitable opportunities.

## Conclusion

Market sentiment has a measurable impact on trading behavior and profitability. Monitoring Fear & Greed conditions can help traders optimize risk management and improve strategy selection.
